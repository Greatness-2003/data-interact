This page displays three interactive visualizations that explore the relationship between voting habits, education, income, and voter turnout in Virginia. The first plot is a parallel categories plot that shows the distribution of voting habits by education and income. The second plot is a bubble plot that shows the relationship between voter turnout, absentee ballots, and total registered voters by locality. The third plot is a choropleth map that shows the percentage of voter turnout in the 2022 general election by locality.

Relationship Between Voting Habits, Education, And Income Levels

Parallel Categories Plot

This plot shows relationship in voting patterns, education and income levels of voters. There are three categories of voting habits: rarely, sporadic and always. The income variable is divided into four: 45-75k, 75.. And education has three levels: highschool, some college, and college graduate. The parallel categories plot shows the counts between all levels of each variable.

Comparison of Voter Turnout And Absentee Voters in Virginia Elections

Bubble Plot

This plot shows data from Virginia. The scatterplot shows percentage of voter turnout on the x-axis and percentage of absentee voters (y-axis) in Virginia from 2014-2022. Each bubble represents a county or city in Virginia. The size of each bubble represents the total number of registered voters in that county or city. Unique colors represent the region of the state where the county is located. This plot has interactive features. Hovering over a bubble will produce some information about the city such as the percent voter turnout, percent absentee ballot, and total voters values.

Distribution of Voter Turnout in 2022 Virginia Elections

Choropleth Map

This map shows data from the 2022 Virginia election. It shows percent voter turnout by county and city in Virginia on a choropleth map. Darker bins indicate higher percentage values.
